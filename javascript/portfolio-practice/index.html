Hi, I'm Richard Maliyetu

I’m a computer science graduate from the University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC), based in Baltimore, Maryland. I’m passionate about turning ideas into functional, user-friendly solutions through code. With experience in teaching, backend development, and full-stack design, I enjoy building projects that solve real problems and create meaningful impact.

About Me

My journey in technology began with a childhood curiosity about how video games were made, which evolved into a deep passion for programming and problem-solving. At UMBC, I worked as a Teaching Assistant for C++, where I strengthened my communication and leadership skills by helping students grasp complex programming concepts. I also participated in the FastTrack’d program, where I was introduced to full-stack development and gained hands-on experience building dynamic web applications. This experience helped me understand the full lifecycle of software development and deepen my interest in creating end-to-end solutions.

My projects — including an Inventory Management System that streamlined student access to campus resources, a Twitter Clone, a backend web service built with Spring Boot, and HobbyMatch, a social app connecting people with shared interests — reflect my enthusiasm for backend development, databases, and full-stack design. As a first-generation college graduate, I’m driven by curiosity, growth, and community. I’m always looking for opportunities to learn, contribute, and build technology that makes a difference.
